From Formulation to Treatment Structure — What Does This Patient’s Data Require?

Assignment Overview

You have completed your diagnostic reasoning and 5 P’s formulation for M.K. Your formulation — particularly the perpetuating factors and predisposing vulnerabilities — points toward DBT as the indicated modality.

In clinical practice, DBT exists in multiple configurations:

  • Comprehensive DBT — Individual DBT therapy + skills group + between-session support + consultation team, integrated as a single treatment system
  • DBT Skills Group + Separate Individual Therapy — The most common real-world configuration. The skills group follows a DBT curriculum, but the individual therapist may or may not use DBT-specific interventions and is typically not coordinated with the group
  • Individual DBT-Informed Therapy — A single clinician using DBT concepts (validation strategies, chain analysis, behavioral targets) without the skills group component

For this assignment, assume that M.K.’s outpatient clinic offers all three configurations. The clinic has peer support specialists who have completed overview training in DBT (familiar with the model, stages, and skills modules) but are not certified DBT providers. They are available for between-session contact during business hours. Cost, insurance, and scheduling are equivalent across options.

Your task: Determine which configuration M.K.’s presentation requires and defend that recommendation using her formulation data. The question is not whether DBT is appropriate — your Part 1 work established that. The question is what M.K.’s specific clinical picture demands from the treatment structure, and why.
