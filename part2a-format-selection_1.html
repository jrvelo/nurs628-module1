You do not need to address all of these. Identify and apply the factors that are most clinically relevant to D.R.

Universality
Recognizing that others share similar feelings, thoughts, and problems. Reduces isolation and shame.
Instillation of Hope
Seeing others at different stages of recovery; believing improvement is possible.
Interpersonal Learning
Gaining insight into one’s own interpersonal patterns through feedback and interaction with group members.
Group Cohesiveness
Sense of belonging, acceptance, and mutual support within the group. Analogous to therapeutic alliance in individual therapy.
Altruism
Helping others in the group; discovering one has something of value to offer. Counteracts feelings of worthlessness.
Corrective Recapitulation
Re-experiencing and reworking family-of-origin dynamics within the group setting.
Socializing Techniques
Developing social skills through group interaction, feedback, and modeling.
Imitative Behavior
Modeling coping strategies and behaviors observed in other group members or the therapist.
Catharsis
Expressing and processing strong emotions within a safe, supportive environment.
Existential Factors
Confronting fundamental issues of responsibility, meaning, and acceptance of life’s limitations.
Imparting Information
Psychoeducation and guidance provided by the therapist or other group members.

Convergent & Discriminant Validity — Applied to Treatment Format

Think about this the same way you evaluated diagnostic evidence in Part 1:

Convergent Targets

Treatment goals that both formats can address. Convergent targets confirm that CBT is the right modality — but they don’t help you choose between formats.

Example: Both individual and group CBT can target automatic thoughts through cognitive restructuring. This is like a transdiagnostic symptom — it supports the diagnosis but doesn’t help you discriminate between two possible diagnoses.
Discriminant Targets

Treatment goals that one format addresses meaningfully better than the other. These are the findings that help you choose.

Example for group: D.R.’s belief that “I’m the only one who struggles like this” — group directly challenges this through universality in a way individual therapy cannot replicate.
Example for individual: Deep-rooted family-of-origin schemas around performance may require the sustained, individualized attention that individual CBT provides.

Identify which of D.R.’s treatment targets converge across formats and which discriminate between them. Use the discriminant targets to support your recommendation.
