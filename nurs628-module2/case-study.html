History of Present Illness

D.R. is a 31-year-old male presenting to the outpatient psychiatric clinic for an initial evaluation, referred by his primary care provider after reporting persistent difficulty with sleep, concentration, and “feeling overwhelmed” at work. This is his first contact with psychiatric services.

Patient reports symptoms began approximately 6 months ago, coinciding with taking on a larger project portfolio at work, and have progressively worsened over the past 8–10 weeks. He identifies no single triggering event. He describes difficulty falling asleep, stating he lies in bed “going over everything I did that day, everything I need to do tomorrow,” and reports waking at 3–4 AM and being unable to return to sleep. He describes persistent difficulty concentrating at work, noting that tasks that previously took him an hour now take most of the morning. He reports low energy and a general sense of heaviness, particularly in the mornings. He states he has been declining social invitations from friends, adding, “I don’t really feel like doing anything after work. I just want to sit there.” He reports reduced enjoyment in activities he previously found pleasurable, including running and cooking, though he still engages in them occasionally.

Patient describes a pattern of spending excessive time reviewing and revising his work before submitting it. He states he will rewrite emails “four or five times” before sending them and reports staying late to check completed projects for errors, stating, “I know it’s probably fine but I keep thinking I missed something.” He was offered a team lead position approximately 3 months ago and declined it, stating, “I didn’t think I was ready. I’d probably just end up proving that I can’t handle it.”

He reports increased irritability, particularly with his girlfriend, which he attributes to “being on edge all the time,” and notes feeling guilty about this afterward. He describes a persistent sense that his performance is inadequate, stating, “Everyone else seems to manage fine. I don’t know what’s wrong with me.”

Symptoms are aggravated by work deadlines, presentations to senior leadership, and performance review periods. Patient also identifies phone calls from his parents as stressful, stating they frequently ask about his career progress and whether he has “thought about going back to school for something more.” He reports some relief on weekends with limited work demands, during time with his girlfriend, and on occasional runs when he can motivate himself to go. He notes that completing a task well provides brief relief, but “it doesn’t last—I just move on to worrying about the next thing.”

Patient has not previously engaged in therapy or taken psychiatric medication. He reports trying melatonin for sleep with minimal benefit. His PCP prescribed hydroxyzine 25 mg PO QHS PRN approximately 3 weeks ago; patient reports it helps him fall asleep initially but does not prevent early morning waking. He rates his overall distress at 7/10. He is maintaining employment and self-care but reports his work quality and interpersonal relationships are suffering, stating, “I’m still showing up but I feel like I’m barely holding it together.”
